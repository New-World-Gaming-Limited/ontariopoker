Overview

GGPoker Ontario holds the top spot in Ontario's regulated poker market by a meaningful margin, earning an overall rating of 9.4 out of 10. Operated by NSUS Limited and fully registered with iGaming Ontario, GGPoker.ca is built on the same award-winning technology that powers the world's largest online poker network by traffic. The platform brings its full global feature set to Ontario's ring-fenced player pool, delivering a product that matches or exceeds what dedicated poker players expect from any major international site. Visit the operator's site for current offer details.

What separates GGPoker from every other Ontario room is the depth of its built-in software tools. Smart HUD displays real-time session statistics on every player at your table - VPIP, PFR, 3-bet percentage, aggression frequencies, and hands played - without requiring any third-party tracking software. PokerCraft goes further, storing permanent personal statistics and providing session-over-session analytics that let players study their own tendencies in detail. Smart Betting streamlines bet sizing with an adaptive interface, Card Squeeze adds a tactile card-reveal animation, and All-In Insurance lets players pay a small premium to guarantee their equity share when the money goes in before all community cards are dealt. Third-party HUDs are banned, but with Smart HUD included free, most players won't miss them.

The Ocean Rewards loyalty program launched on January 30, 2026, replacing the Fish Buffet system. It runs eight tiers - Fish, Shrimp, Crab, Turtle, Octopus, Dolphin, Whale, and Shark - with cashback rates climbing with rates that improve at higher tiers. Players earn 100 Tide Points and 100 GEMs per dollar of rake contributed. GEMs convert to cash at rates that improve with volume, starting at 1,000 GEMs per dollar at the base level. Tier protection runs for the full calendar year, meaning no demotions once a level is reached.

The tournament ecosystem is the deepest in Ontario's regulated market. GGPoker holds an active WSOP Online partnership, running dozens of bracelet events annually with guaranteed prize pools that dwarf anything else available to Ontario players. The recurring microFestival series targets smaller bankrolls, making festival-style play accessible from the micro stakes. Daily events run nearly every hour with buy-ins starting from C$1, and a full satellite network feeds into all major events. The Spin & Gold jackpot sit-and-go format completes the picture for players who want a quick high-variance session.

GGPoker Ontario is regulated by the Alcohol and Gaming Commission of Ontario (AGCO) and operates under iGaming Ontario as a registered operator. The ring-fenced Ontario player pool means you compete against other Ontario players rather than the global GGPoker network, which keeps traffic at moderate but consistent levels. Cash games run reliably at micro and low stakes throughout the day, with peak activity in the evening Eastern time. No Limit Hold'em runs from NL2 through NL20000, PLO from PLO20 through PLO20000, and Short Deck Hold'em is available from NL10 upward. The 9-max format was expanded in 2023, adding tables from NL2 through NL25 alongside the existing 6-max offering.

Software and App

GGPoker's client earns the highest software rating among Ontario's six regulated rooms at 9.6 out of 10. The desktop client is available for Windows and Mac, with dedicated iOS and Android apps that maintain full feature parity including Smart HUD, PokerCraft, and all game formats. Multi-tabling supports up to four tables simultaneously on mobile, with no practical limit on desktop. The interface is polished and responsive, with table themes, card customization, and felt colors adjustable per player preference. The lobby filters by game type, stakes, and format, making it straightforward to find the right table quickly.

Smart HUD is the feature that most distinguishes GGPoker's client from every competitor in the Ontario market. It shows each opponent's VPIP, PFR, three-bet percentage, aggression factor, attempt-to-steal percentage, and total hands played, all visible directly on the table without any setup. Clicking a player's avatar surfaces additional stats including c-bet frequencies and showdown tendencies, plus their recent hand history against you. These statistics reset between sessions - they don't carry over - so the HUD reflects current session patterns rather than a long-term database. PokerCraft, by contrast, stores cumulative personal data permanently, giving players a durable record of their own results, positional statistics, and hand histories that can be reviewed and filtered at any time.

All-In Insurance and EV Cashout are risk management features that no other Ontario room offers. All-In Insurance allows a player who is all-in before the board is complete to pay a premium and lock in a guaranteed portion of the pot based on their equity at the time of going all-in. EV Cashout (1% fee for standard games, 2% for 6-card PLO) lets players request the mathematical expected value of a hand before it plays out, eliminating variance entirely at the cost of the small fee. Run It Twice and Run It Three Times are also available in cash games, giving players additional options for managing variance in large pots. Bomb Pots add a variant where all players post before the hand and go directly to the flop, creating action-heavy community pots.

Rush & Cash is GGPoker's fast-fold cash game format, folding players instantly to a new table with a fresh hand. It's available across NL Hold'em and PLO stakes and is particularly well-suited to mobile play, where the single-hand interface and portrait orientation make it easy to get through high volumes of hands in a short session. No rake is collected on Rush & Cash tables that aren't full, which benefits players at off-peak hours. Spin & Gold jackpot sit-and-gos run three-handed with randomly generated prize pool multipliers up to 1,200 times the buy-in, and the format feeds into Spin & Gold leaderboards that add an additional competitive layer beyond the individual game results.

Tournament Ecosystem

GGPoker Ontario's tournament schedule runs nearly around the clock. Buy-ins start from C$1 and the daily guaranteed schedule includes a Daily Main Event, Daily Hyper, Daily Turbo, Daily Big, and Daily Deep Stack formats, with Sunday guarantees roughly doubling. The total weekly guarantee across all events reaches approximately C$8 million on the global platform, though Ontario-specific guarantees are smaller given the ring-fenced pool. Satellites into all major events run from C$1, making even the high-stakes series accessible to players with modest bankrolls.

The WSOP Online partnership is GGPoker's most distinctive tournament feature. In 2025, GGPoker ran 33 WSOP Online bracelet events from August 17 through September 30, with over C$63 million guaranteed across the series and a C$25 million Main Event. Ontario players can compete in these events through the ring-fenced client, though the Ontario pool for side events is separate from the global field. WSOP Super Circuit events run year-round as a second tier, bringing World Series of Poker branding and structured series play to the Ontario schedule on a recurring basis.

The GGMasters serves as GGPoker's flagship weekly event globally, with Ontario players competing in their own ring-fenced equivalent. High Roller series and Mystery Millions bounty events add premium options at the upper end of the buy-in range. Flip & Go tournaments, which begin as an all-in lottery format and transition to a standard final table structure, provide a unique hybrid format that appeals to players who want both the lottery element and traditional deep-stack play.

The microFestival series is a GGPoker Ontario exclusive concept, designed specifically for players at the micro stakes who want the experience of playing a festival without needing a large bankroll. GGPoker markets it as Ontario's largest micro-stakes poker festival. The combination of WSOP branding at the high end, microFestival at the low end, and a consistent daily schedule in between gives tournament players across all bankroll levels more options than any other Ontario room can offer.

Cash Game Formats

GGPoker Ontario's cash game lobby covers more variants and stake levels than any other room in the province. No Limit Hold'em runs in 6-max and 9-max formats from NL2 (C$0.01/C$0.02) through NL20000 (C$100/C$200). The 9-max format was expanded in 2023 and runs from NL2 through NL25, adding a full-ring option for players who prefer a tighter starting hand requirement and lower average pot sizes. PLO runs from PLO20 through PLO20000 in standard 4-card format, with 5-card PLO and 6-card PLO also available at higher stakes. Short Deck Hold'em (also called 6+ Hold'em) runs from NL10 upward and uses a button ante structure where a flush beats a full house.

Rush & Cash is the fast-fold variant, available across NL Hold'em and PLO stakes at the micro through mid levels. Players fold and are immediately moved to a new table and dealt a new hand, eliminating idle time between hands and increasing hourly volume significantly. The format is particularly popular among players who want to put in high volumes in limited time and is well-optimized for mobile sessions. All-In Insurance and EV Cashout are available in all cash game variants, offering unique risk management tools not found elsewhere in the Ontario market.

Cash game traffic in Ontario's ring-fenced pool is most reliable at the micro and low stakes, with NL2 through NL25 running consistently throughout the day and NL50 through NL200 available during peak hours. Short Deck and PLO5/PLO6 have thinner tables and are best played during evening peak hours. Smart HUD and PokerCraft provide cash game players with the analytical tools to track win rates by position, stake, and format over time, giving GGPoker a meaningful edge for players who take a data-driven approach to improving their results. Rake runs at 5% flat on all cash game formats, with caps ranging from C$0.20 at NL2 6-max to C$15 at NL2000+.

Rewards and Loyalty

The Ocean Rewards program launched January 30, 2026, replacing the Fish Buffet system that had been in place since GGPoker's early days. The program uses a tiered system. Visit https://www.ggpoker.ca for current tier details and cashback rates.

GEMs are the primary currency for converting play into cash. GEMs convert to cash at rates that improve with volume. Check https://www.ggpoker.ca for current conversion rates. GEMs can also be exchanged for tournament tickets, bonuses, or store items in the GGPoker rewards shop. Tide Points contribute to weekly leaderboards and Fish Buffet-style missions that provide supplemental rewards beyond the core cashback structure.

The Maple Rewards program is exclusive to Ontario players and runs separately from the global Ocean Rewards system. It delivers monthly promotional offers - freerolls, ticket bundles, cashback boosts, and limited-time missions - that are not available on GGPoker's global client. GGPoker markets Maple Rewards with a monthly rotating schedule, and players should check the Promotions section of GGPoker.ca regularly as offers change each month. The Honeymoon for Newcomers program guides new players through a 30-day structured mission set during their first month, providing rewards for exploring formats like Rush & Cash, Spin & Gold, and the tournament lobby.

Deposits and Withdrawals

GGPoker Ontario processes deposits through Interac, Visa, Mastercard, and MuchBetter. The minimum deposit is C$10 and all transactions are denominated in Canadian dollars. Interac e-Transfer is the most popular method for Ontario players, connecting directly to Canadian bank accounts with near-instant processing. MuchBetter processes deposits instantly and is the fastest e-wallet option. Visa and Mastercard deposits are typically processed within a few minutes. There are no GGPoker-side deposit fees, though card issuers may apply their own transaction charges.

Withdrawals are processed through Interac, MuchBetter, and bank transfer. Processing time is typically within 24 hours for most methods, with bank transfers requiring an additional one to three business days for funds to appear in the account. A C$1 fee applies per withdrawal. Withdrawals above C$3,000 may require additional documentation as part of the operator's compliance procedures. The minimum withdrawal amount is C$10 on most methods. eWallets including MuchBetter are often processed same-day.

For information about how the new-player offer interacts with withdrawals, check the offer terms directly on https://www.ggpoker.ca. Under AGCO rules, we cannot detail bonus mechanics here.

Geolocation and Eligibility

To play at GGPoker Ontario, you must be 19 years of age or older and physically located within the province of Ontario at the time of play. The platform uses geolocation technology to verify your position, which means VPN use will prevent you from accessing the site. GGPoker operates under the oversight of the Alcohol and Gaming Commission of Ontario (AGCO) and iGaming Ontario (iGO), the provincial regulator and conductor respectively. Your account, funds, and personal data are protected under Ontario's regulated framework.

Customer Support and Disputes

GGPoker offers 24/7 Live Chat, Email for player inquiries. Response times vary by channel, with live chat typically providing the fastest resolution. For account-related issues, identity verification, or withdrawal inquiries, email support may be more appropriate as it allows you to attach documentation.

If you have a complaint that cannot be resolved directly with GGPoker, you have the right to escalate it to iGaming Ontario (iGO). The iGO complaint process provides an independent review channel for disputes involving registered operators. Details on how to file a complaint are available on the iGO website.
