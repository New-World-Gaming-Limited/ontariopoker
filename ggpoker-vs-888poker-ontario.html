Overview: GGPoker vs 888poker in Ontario

GGPoker and 888poker are the two highest-rated regulated poker rooms in Ontario, and both have built loyal player bases in the province. GGPoker leads our rankings with a 9.4/10.0 rating, while 888poker follows at 9.0/10.0. This head-to-head comparison examines where each room excels and why GGPoker takes the overall edge.

Player Pool and Traffic

GGPoker holds the largest standalone player pool among Ontario's regulated rooms. Peak hour traffic is consistently higher across all stake levels, which translates to shorter wait times and bigger tournament fields. You'll find action at micro, low, and mid stakes throughout the day.

888poker's Ontario traffic is respectable and has grown steadily. The room fills tables well at lower stakes and during peak hours, though it can't match GGPoker's volume at mid stakes. 888poker's player base tends to skew slightly more recreational, which some grinders consider a plus.

Software and Features

GGPoker's software is the most feature-packed client in Ontario. The Smart HUD provides free real-time stats on opponents, while PokerCraft delivers detailed analytics for reviewing your play. All-in Insurance, straddle options, and Run It Twice round out a toolset that no other Ontario room matches. Third-party HUDs aren't allowed, which keeps the playing field level.

888poker's client is clean, reliable, and easy to navigate. Its standout features include BLAST, a fast-paced jackpot sit-and-go format where tournaments last just a few minutes, and webcam tables where you can see your opponents via video. Snap Poker is 888poker's fast-fold cash game option. The software does its job well, but it doesn't offer the same depth of built-in tools as GGPoker.

Tournament Schedule

GGPoker runs the stronger daily tournament schedule in Ontario. Guarantee values are typically higher, fields are larger, and the variety of formats and buy-in levels gives tournament players more options. The room's Spin & Gold jackpot sit-and-gos are a popular quick-play alternative to traditional MTTs.

888poker offers a solid tournament lineup of its own, with a good mix of buy-in levels and formats. The room regularly runs special series and promotions that boost tournament activity. For pure tournament volume, though, GGPoker has the edge.

Cash Games

Cash game players will find more tables and faster seating at GGPoker. The room fills games across a wider range of stakes, from the lowest micro levels up through mid stakes. Rush & Cash provides a smooth fast-fold experience for players who want high hand volume.

888poker's cash game traffic is decent at lower stakes. Snap Poker is a well-implemented fast-fold option. Table selection is more limited at mid stakes and above compared to GGPoker, but the player pool's recreational leaning can mean softer games at the stakes where tables do run.

Mobile App

GGPoker's mobile app is one of the best in online poker. It was built mobile-first, and the full suite of features works seamlessly on phone or tablet. Multi-tabling, Smart HUD, and PokerCraft are all accessible on the go. The interface is responsive and well-designed.

888poker's mobile app covers the essentials and lets you play cash games, tournaments, and BLAST on your device. It's functional and stable. However, the user experience isn't as polished as GGPoker's, and the app doesn't support the same depth of features on smaller screens.

Payments and Withdrawals

888poker has a slight edge in payment options, accepting a broad range of methods including Visa, Mastercard, Interac, ecoPayz, MuchBetter, and Apple Pay. Withdrawal processing is efficient, with e-wallets typically processed within 24 hours.

GGPoker covers all the major methods Ontario players need, including Visa, Mastercard, Interac, and several e-wallets. Processing times are similar to 888poker's. Both rooms handle payments reliably.

Customer Support

888poker provides 24/7 live chat and email support with good response times. Our testing found 888poker's support agents to be knowledgeable and helpful. GGPoker also offers 24/7 live chat and email. Both rooms perform well in this category, though 888poker's slightly longer track record in customer service gives it a narrow advantage here.

Rewards Programs

GGPoker's Fish Buffet program rewards consistent play with cash prizes at progressively higher tiers. The GGCare system adds random cashback that can benefit any player, even those at lower volumes. The rewards structure is transparent and offers genuine value.

888poker's Made To Play program offers daily challenges, freeroll entries, and cashback rewards. It's well-designed for recreational players who enjoy varied daily objectives. The program is engaging, though the raw cash value tends to be lower than GGPoker's Fish Buffet for regular players.

Our Verdict: GGPoker Wins

After comparing every major category, GGPoker comes out ahead of 888poker in Ontario. The larger player pool means more games running at more stakes. The software is more innovative, with built-in tools that other rooms simply don't offer. The tournament schedule is deeper, the mobile app is better, and the rewards program delivers solid value.

888poker is a legitimate, regulated room with its own strengths, and there's nothing wrong with playing there. But if you're choosing one room as your primary Ontario poker site, GGPoker is the stronger pick.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GGPoker better than 888poker in Ontario?

GGPoker rates higher overall due to its larger player pool, more advanced software features, and stronger tournament schedule. 888poker is still a strong room, particularly for its BLAST format and webcam tables.

Which room has better software?

GGPoker's software is more feature-rich, with Smart HUD, PokerCraft analytics, and All-in Insurance built in. 888poker's client is clean and reliable, and offers unique features like webcam tables and BLAST jackpot SNGs.

Can I play at both GGPoker and 888poker in Ontario?

Yes. Both are fully licensed by the AGCO and you can maintain accounts at both. Many Ontario players keep accounts at multiple rooms to take advantage of different features and promotions.

Which room is better for recreational players?

Both rooms are good choices for casual players. 888poker's Made To Play promotions and BLAST format are fun for recreational play, while GGPoker's Spin & Gold jackpot SNGs and GGCare cashback also appeal to casual players.
