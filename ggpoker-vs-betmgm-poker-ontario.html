Overview: GGPoker vs BetMGM Poker in Ontario

GGPoker and BetMGM Poker represent different approaches to the Ontario market. GGPoker is a poker-first platform built around innovative features and the province's largest standalone player pool. BetMGM Poker leverages the MGM brand, a shared network with PartyPoker and Bwin, and cross-platform integration with its sportsbook and casino. Here's how they compare across the categories that matter.

Player Pool and Traffic

GGPoker's standalone Ontario player pool is the largest of any regulated room in the province. Cash game traffic peaks higher, and tournament fields are consistently bigger. You'll find games running across more stake levels throughout the day and night.

BetMGM Poker shares its player pool with PartyPoker and Bwin, which helps boost its overall traffic. The combined network provides reasonable action at low and mid stakes. However, even with the shared pool, total traffic doesn't reach GGPoker's levels. The network does have the advantage of pooling players from three different brands, which helps during off-peak hours.

Software and Features

GGPoker's software leads Ontario's market with its built-in Smart HUD, PokerCraft analytics, All-in Insurance, and Run It Twice. These features give players a significant edge in studying their game and managing variance, all without needing third-party software. The client is modern, responsive, and regularly updated with new features.

BetMGM Poker runs on a client shared with PartyPoker. It's a functional, stable piece of software that handles the basics well. Table customization options exist, and the interface is clean. But it lacks the depth of built-in tools that GGPoker offers. There's no integrated HUD or analytics suite, and the feature set feels more traditional compared to GGPoker's innovation.

Tournament Schedule

GGPoker runs the more extensive tournament schedule, with a wider range of buy-in levels, bigger guaranteed prize pools, and more varied formats. Daily MTTs, Spin & Gold jackpot sit-and-gos, and regular special series keep tournament players busy.

BetMGM Poker offers a respectable tournament lineup with solid guarantees, especially on weekends and during promotional series. The shared network helps fill fields. For players who play a moderate tournament volume, BetMGM provides enough variety, but serious grinders will find more options on GGPoker.

Cash Games

GGPoker fills cash game tables across more stake levels than BetMGM. Rush & Cash provides fast-fold action for high hand-per-hour players. The room's traffic advantage means less time on wait lists and better table selection at any given time.

BetMGM Poker's cash games run on the shared network, which helps maintain action at popular stakes. The experience is solid at low-stakes No-Limit Hold'em, though table selection narrows at higher levels. Players who also use BetMGM's sportsbook may appreciate having everything under one account.

Mobile App

GGPoker's mobile-first design philosophy gives it a clear advantage. The app is fast, full-featured, and comfortable to use for extended sessions. Multi-tabling on mobile works well, and all the room's distinctive features carry over from desktop.

BetMGM Poker's mobile app is adequate for basic play. It handles cash games and tournaments without issues, but the experience feels less refined than GGPoker's. The app's interface is shared with the broader BetMGM platform, which means it's designed to serve poker, sports, and casino, rather than being optimized solely for poker.

Payments and Withdrawals

Both rooms accept the standard Ontario payment methods, including Visa, Mastercard, Interac, and popular e-wallets. Processing times are comparable. BetMGM has the convenience factor of a shared wallet across its poker, sportsbook, and casino products, which some players find useful.

Customer Support

GGPoker provides 24/7 live chat and email support with generally quick response times. BetMGM offers live chat and email as well, with the added benefit of a robust help center. Both rooms handle support adequately, with BetMGM's broader brand infrastructure providing a slight edge in documentation and self-service options.

Rewards Programs

The rewards comparison here is interesting because the two programs serve different purposes. GGPoker's Fish Buffet is a straightforward poker rewards program that pays cash based on volume. It's transparent and offers good value for regular players.

BetMGM Poker's MGM Rewards integration is unique in Ontario's market. Poker play earns tier credits and points that can be redeemed at MGM resort properties worldwide. For players who visit MGM casinos, this cross-platform loyalty has real value. For pure online poker players, GGPoker's cash-based rewards are more directly useful.

Our Verdict: GGPoker Wins

After comparing every major category, GGPoker comes out ahead of BetMGM Poker in Ontario. The larger player pool means more games running at more stakes. The software is more innovative, with built-in tools that other rooms simply don't offer. The tournament schedule is deeper, the mobile app is better, and the rewards program delivers solid value.

BetMGM Poker is a legitimate, regulated room with its own strengths, and there's nothing wrong with playing there. But if you're choosing one room as your primary Ontario poker site, GGPoker is the stronger pick.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GGPoker better than BetMGM Poker in Ontario?

GGPoker earns a higher rating overall thanks to its larger standalone player pool, more innovative software features, and superior mobile app. BetMGM Poker is a solid choice if you value its shared network and MGM Rewards integration.

Does BetMGM Poker share players with other rooms?

Yes. BetMGM Poker shares its Ontario player pool with PartyPoker and Bwin, all operating on the same network. This gives BetMGM players access to a combined pool, though the total still doesn't match GGPoker's standalone traffic.

Which room has better tournaments?

GGPoker offers a larger and more varied daily tournament schedule with bigger guaranteed prize pools. BetMGM Poker runs solid tournaments with respectable guarantees, particularly on weekends.

Can I earn MGM Rewards points playing poker at BetMGM?

Yes. BetMGM Poker Ontario is integrated with the MGM Rewards loyalty program, allowing you to earn tier credits and points that can be redeemed at MGM properties. GGPoker has its own Fish Buffet rewards system that pays out in cash.
