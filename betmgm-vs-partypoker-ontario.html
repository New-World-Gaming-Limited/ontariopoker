Key fact: BetMGM and PartyPoker share the same player pool

BetMGM Poker and PartyPoker Ontario run on the ElectraWorks Maple / BetMGM network. Both brands share the same cash game tables and tournament pools. The difference between them is the app, the rewards structure, and the branding. Choosing one over the other doesn't affect the games you play. It affects how you're rewarded for playing.

Overview: Same Network, Different Experience

This comparison has an unusual starting point: BetMGM Poker and PartyPoker Ontario are competing products that draw from the same player pool. Both brands sit on the ElectraWorks Maple network, which means when you sit at a C$0.50/C$1 cash game table on BetMGM, you may be playing against someone logged in through PartyPoker. The fields in their shared tournaments overlap completely.

The practical consequence is that your choice between these two rooms has nothing to do with game availability, traffic, or stake levels. It's entirely about software preference, rewards structure, and offer terms. BetMGM Canada Inc and ElectraWorks Maple Limited are separate AGCO-registered operators, but they run shared infrastructure in Ontario.

Software

BetMGM Poker's client is the cleaner of the two. The lobby is easy to navigate, table registration is straightforward, and the interface behaves consistently on both desktop and mobile. FastForward, BetMGM's fast-fold cash game format, works well for players who want to maximize hands per hour without waiting for tables. The overall experience feels polished relative to competitors.

PartyPoker's client has gone through multiple redesigns in recent years. The current Ontario version is functional but has a slightly more cluttered feel than BetMGM's. SPINS, PartyPoker's jackpot sit-and-go format, is the standout feature, offering fast-paced play with a randomly assigned prize pool. If SPINS is your format of choice, PartyPoker is the natural home for it. For everything else, BetMGM's client is smoother to use.

Tournaments

Because both rooms share the same tournament pools, the MTT schedule and prize guarantees are identical. A tournament visible in the BetMGM lobby and one visible in the PartyPoker lobby at the same stake level are the same event. You can register through either app. For tournament players, this means the choice of room has no bearing on the tournaments you can access.

The difference shows up in format-specific features. BetMGM's FastForward is a cash game format, not a tournament format. PartyPoker's SPINS are quick jackpot sit-and-gos that don't overlap with the shared MTT pool. These formats are exclusive to their respective apps and give players a reason to maintain accounts at both.

Cash Games

The cash game tables are shared across the network. Players on BetMGM and PartyPoker compete together at every stake level. Traffic levels, table selection, and wait times are identical regardless of which app you use to access the games. The only cash-game-specific differentiation is FastForward on BetMGM, which is available exclusively through that client. PartyPoker doesn't offer an equivalent fast-fold cash game format.

Traffic

The ElectraWorks Maple network holds a single combined player pool for Ontario. Both BetMGM and PartyPoker draw from that same pool, so the effective traffic figure applies equally to both. Players logging in through BetMGM see the same number of cash game tables and tournament fields as those logging in through PartyPoker. This is a meaningful distinction from all other comparison pairs on this site, where each room has its own independent player pool.

Rake

Rake rates are set at the network level and apply uniformly to both BetMGM and PartyPoker players. Both charge 5% rake with a C$3 cap at standard cash game stakes in Ontario. There is no advantage to choosing one room over the other based on rake cost.

Rewards

This is where the two rooms genuinely diverge. BetMGM's Grind Rewards program uses a BRP (Bonus Reward Points) ladder system. Players accumulate points through rake and tournament fees, progressing through clearly defined tiers with tangible milestone rewards. The structure is transparent and allows players to plan how many sessions they need to reach the next tier.

PartyPoker's cashback rewards system is more opaque. Cashback percentages and qualifying conditions are less clearly presented, and players have reported inconsistency in how credits are applied. The system delivers value for active players but requires more attention to extract full value from it. For players who prefer knowing exactly what they're earning, BetMGM's BRP structure is easier to track.

Freerolls

PartyPoker runs a daily C$250 freeroll, which gives casual players more frequent free-entry opportunities than BetMGM's weekly C$500 freeroll. On a per-week basis, PartyPoker offers seven freeroll entries versus BetMGM's one, though BetMGM's single event carries the larger individual prize pool. For players who want daily no-cost play, PartyPoker wins this narrow category. Both rooms also run SPINS freerolls through their respective jackpot sit-and-go formats.

Who Should Choose Which

Choose BetMGM Poker if you...

  • Prefer a structured, transparent rewards ladder (Grind Rewards BRP)
  • Want the cleaner, easier-to-navigate client
  • Play FastForward fast-fold cash games
  • Value clear offer terms with no surprises
  • Want the higher-value weekly freeroll (C$500)
  • Are new to the ElectraWorks Maple network

Choose PartyPoker if you...

  • Love SPINS jackpot sit-and-gos as your primary format
  • Want daily freeroll access (C$250 every day)
  • Prefer a cashback-style rewards model
  • Already have a PartyPoker account and know the client
  • Want to run accounts at both to access SPINS alongside FastForward

Running Both Accounts

Because BetMGM and PartyPoker share the same game pool, running accounts at both has no downside. You won't be splitting your rake or dividing your attention across separate player pools. You'll be playing the same games regardless of which app you log in through, while having access to FastForward through BetMGM and SPINS through PartyPoker. For players who want flexibility across formats, maintaining both accounts makes practical sense.

Bwin is a third brand on the same network and also shares the ElectraWorks Maple player pool. Ontario players who want access to all three skins' exclusive features can maintain all three accounts while playing in the same game pool throughout.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do BetMGM and PartyPoker share the same poker tables in Ontario?

Yes. BetMGM Poker and PartyPoker Ontario both operate on the ElectraWorks Maple network and share the same cash game tables and tournament pools. When you sit at a table through either app, you may be seated against players logged in through the other brand. The player pools are not separated.

Which has better rewards, BetMGM or PartyPoker?

BetMGM's Grind Rewards BRP ladder is more structured and transparent than PartyPoker's cashback system. BetMGM players can see exactly what rake earns them and track progress toward milestone rewards. PartyPoker's cashback delivers value but is less predictable and harder to track. For most players, BetMGM offers a clearer rewards proposition.

Can I play on both BetMGM and PartyPoker in Ontario at the same time?

Yes, you can hold accounts at both rooms simultaneously. Because they share the same game pool, you won't be playing against different opponents. You will gain access to formats exclusive to each app, such as FastForward on BetMGM and SPINS on PartyPoker. Running both accounts is a common choice for Ontario players on this network.

Which room is better for tournaments, BetMGM or PartyPoker?

For standard MTTs and tournament play, there is no practical difference. Both rooms access the same tournament pools on the ElectraWorks Maple network. Fields, guarantee amounts, and schedules are identical. The distinction is in format-specific options: BetMGM offers FastForward cash game speed play, and PartyPoker offers SPINS jackpot sit-and-gos. Choose based on which format appeals to you.
