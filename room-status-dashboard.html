How to Use This Dashboard

This dashboard shows typical traffic patterns for Ontario's regulated poker rooms. The data reflects general trends rather than real-time table counts, since the Ontario market operates as a ring-fenced ecosystem with its own player pool.

The status indicators on each room card update automatically based on the current Eastern Time. "Peak Hours" means the room is at its busiest, "Active" means solid table availability, and "Quiet" means fewer tables running.

Use the traffic heatmap to plan your sessions around the busiest times. Higher traffic means more table selection and faster tournament fills, but also tougher competition at higher stakes.

The stake availability matrix shows which stakes run consistently at each room. "Limited" means tables may only fill during peak hours. Check the Traffic Index for more detailed player count estimates.

Frequently Asked Questions

The best time for cash games is between 7 PM and 11 PM ET on weekdays, with peak traffic on Sundays from 2 PM to midnight ET. Tournament players should look for Sunday major events starting around 1 PM ET. Micro and low stakes tables run around the clock, but mid-stakes games fill fastest during evening hours.
GGPoker Ontario and PokerStars Ontario consistently have the highest traffic among Ontario-regulated rooms. GGPoker offers the largest tournament schedule including Sunday Majors, while PokerStars has strong cash game traffic especially during evening hours. Both rooms offer games from micro to mid stakes.
Ontario's regulated poker market supports active tables across all six rooms during peak hours. Micro and low-stakes No-Limit Hold'em cash games run consistently throughout the day. Higher stakes and PLO games have more limited availability and are best played during evening peak hours. Tournament schedules run daily across all rooms.
GGPoker and PokerStars offer PLO cash games from micro to low stakes. 888poker, BetMGM, PartyPoker, and Bwin offer limited PLO options, primarily at micro stakes during peak hours. PLO tournament availability is also limited in the Ontario market.
NLH cash games range from $0.01/$0.02 (micro) up to $2/$5 or $5/$10 at the larger rooms like GGPoker and PokerStars. Most rooms reliably spread micro ($0.01/$0.02 to $0.05/$0.10) and low ($0.10/$0.25 to $0.25/$0.50) stakes. Mid stakes ($0.50/$1 to $2/$5) are available at GGPoker and PokerStars during peak hours.
