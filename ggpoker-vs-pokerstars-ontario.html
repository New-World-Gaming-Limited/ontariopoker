Overview: GGPoker vs PokerStars in Ontario

GGPoker and PokerStars are two of the most recognized names in online poker, and both hold AGCO registrations in Ontario. While PokerStars carries decades of global brand equity, GGPoker has built the largest regulated player pool in the province since launching in 2022. This comparison breaks down how they stack up across the categories that matter most to Ontario players in 2026.

Player Pool and Traffic

GGPoker leads Ontario in peak concurrent cash game players at every stake level we track. The room's standalone Ontario pool regularly outperforms PokerStars, which operates a ring-fenced Ontario environment separate from its global network. For cash game grinders and tournament players who want shorter wait times and bigger fields, GGPoker is the stronger choice.

PokerStars still draws healthy traffic for lower-stakes games, but peak numbers at mid and high stakes lag behind GGPoker's. The difference is especially noticeable during evening hours, when GGPoker's Ontario tables fill up faster.

Software and Features

GGPoker's software is modern and feature-rich. The built-in Smart HUD gives every player access to real-time stats without needing third-party tools, which aren't allowed on the platform. PokerCraft provides detailed session analytics, hand histories, and performance graphs. All-in Insurance lets players reduce variance on big all-in spots by paying a small premium. Straddle options and Run It Twice add more flexibility at cash game tables.

PokerStars offers a mature, stable client that has been refined over 20 years. It's fast, reliable, and highly customizable in terms of table layout and appearance. However, it doesn't include any built-in HUD or analytics tools. Players who want that data need to purchase separate HUD software. PokerStars does offer Zoom Poker, its popular fast-fold format, which has loyal fans.

On balance, GGPoker delivers more value out of the box. The Smart HUD and PokerCraft suite are genuinely useful tools that would cost money on any other platform.

Tournament Schedule

Both rooms run strong daily tournament schedules, but GGPoker's Ontario offerings tend to carry larger guarantees and attract bigger fields. Multi-table tournament grinders will find more volume on GGPoker, particularly in the evenings and on weekends.

GGPoker's signature Spin & Gold jackpot sit-and-gos are another popular format that PokerStars doesn't match directly. PokerStars counters with its long-running Sunday tournament series and occasional special event schedules, but the day-to-day schedule is thinner in Ontario compared to GGPoker's.

Cash Games

GGPoker fills tables faster at more stake levels in Ontario. Cash game players benefit from the room's larger pool, which means less time waiting for a seat and more table selection at micro, low, and mid stakes. GGPoker also offers Rush & Cash (its fast-fold variant), PokerCraft tracking for cash game sessions, and optional straddles.

PokerStars' Ontario cash games run at lower and micro stakes, with Zoom Poker offering a convenient fast-fold option. The action is reliable but thinner. High-stakes tables above NL200 are harder to fill on PokerStars Ontario compared to GGPoker.

Mobile App

GGPoker was designed with a mobile-first philosophy, and it shows. The app runs smoothly on both iOS and Android, with full feature parity to the desktop client. Multi-tabling, the Smart HUD, and PokerCraft all work on mobile. The interface is clean and responsive, even on smaller screens.

PokerStars' mobile app is functional and covers the basics well, including Zoom Poker. But it feels dated compared to GGPoker's and lacks the same depth of features on smaller devices. Multi-tabling is possible but less fluid.

Payments and Withdrawals

Both rooms accept Visa, Mastercard, Interac, and major e-wallets. Processing times are similar, with e-wallet withdrawals typically arriving within 24 to 48 hours. GGPoker's payment experience is smooth, though PokerStars has a slightly wider range of accepted methods including some niche options.

Customer Support

GGPoker offers 24/7 live chat and email support, with consistently quicker response times based on our testing. PokerStars provides email-based support and a help center. While PokerStars' responses are accurate, they tend to be slower, sometimes taking 12 to 24 hours for an initial reply.

Rewards Programs

GGPoker's Fish Buffet rewards program and GGCare system offer solid value for regular players. You earn points by playing, and the Fish Buffet provides cash rewards at different tiers. The GGCare program adds random cashback rewards.

PokerStars' Stars Rewards has gone through several iterations over the years and currently offers chests earned through play. The program is adequate but generally considered less generous than GGPoker's Fish Buffet, especially for mid-volume players.

Our Verdict: GGPoker Wins

After comparing every major category, GGPoker comes out ahead of PokerStars in Ontario. The larger player pool means more games running at more stakes. The software is more innovative, with built-in tools that other rooms simply don't offer. The tournament schedule is deeper, the mobile app is better, and the rewards program delivers solid value.

PokerStars is a legitimate, regulated room with its own strengths, and there's nothing wrong with playing there. But if you're choosing one room as your primary Ontario poker site, GGPoker is the stronger pick.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GGPoker or PokerStars better in Ontario?

GGPoker edges ahead overall thanks to its larger Ontario player pool, built-in Smart HUD, and stronger mobile app. PokerStars is still a solid choice for its game variety and Zoom Poker format.

Which room has more traffic in Ontario?

GGPoker consistently reports higher peak cash game traffic across all stake levels in Ontario. PokerStars, while globally large, operates a smaller ring-fenced Ontario pool.

Are both rooms regulated in Ontario?

Yes. Both GGPoker and PokerStars are fully regulated by the Alcohol and Gaming Commission of Ontario (AGCO) and operate through iGaming Ontario.

Do I need a paid HUD for GGPoker Ontario?

No. GGPoker includes a free Smart HUD and PokerCraft analytics suite. Third-party HUDs are not permitted. PokerStars allows third-party HUDs, but they require separate purchase.
