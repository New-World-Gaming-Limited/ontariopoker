Overview: GGPoker vs PartyPoker in Ontario

GGPoker and PartyPoker are both AGCO-regulated Ontario poker rooms, but the gap between them is wider than any other comparison on this page. GGPoker rates 4.7/5.0 to PartyPoker's 3.9/5.0. This isn't a close contest, though PartyPoker does have a few areas where it holds its own. Here's the full breakdown.

Player Pool and Traffic

GGPoker dominates this category. Its standalone Ontario player pool is the largest in the province by a comfortable margin. Cash game tables fill quickly across a broad range of stakes, and tournament fields are consistently strong.

PartyPoker shares its player pool with BetMGM Poker and Bwin, which helps offset its smaller standalone audience. The combined network provides decent action at lower stakes and during peak hours. Off-peak, though, finding games above micro stakes can be challenging. GGPoker's traffic advantage is substantial and consistent.

Software and Features

GGPoker's software is a generation ahead. Smart HUD, PokerCraft analytics, All-in Insurance, Run It Twice, straddle options, and Spin & Gold are all built into the client. The interface is modern, well-organized, and regularly updated. It's the most feature-complete poker software available in Ontario.

PartyPoker's client is dated in comparison. It handles the fundamentals, and fastforward (its fast-fold format) works well. But the overall experience feels less polished. There's no built-in HUD or analytics suite. Table customization options are limited. The software works, but it doesn't impress.

Tournament Schedule

GGPoker runs a much larger daily tournament schedule with bigger guarantees, more buy-in levels, and more formats. Whether you're looking for micro-stakes MTTs, mid-stakes events, or jackpot sit-and-gos, GGPoker has more volume on any given day.

PartyPoker's tournament offerings are modest by comparison. The shared network helps fill fields, and the room runs some decent weekend events. But the daily schedule is thin, and guarantee values are lower. Tournament-focused players will find significantly more value on GGPoker.

Cash Games

GGPoker fills tables at more stakes, more often. The room's traffic ensures you can find a game at almost any time, from the lowest micro stakes through mid-stakes games. Rush & Cash is a well-implemented fast-fold option for players who want maximum hands per hour.

PartyPoker's cash games run primarily at lower stakes. The shared network with BetMGM and Bwin helps, and fastforward provides a fast-fold alternative. But table selection is noticeably more limited, especially at stakes above NL50. If cash games are your primary format, GGPoker is the clear choice.

Mobile App

GGPoker's mobile app is among the best in the industry. It's fast, feature-rich, and designed for serious mobile play. Multi-tabling, Smart HUD, and PokerCraft all work on mobile devices. The interface adapts well to smaller screens without sacrificing functionality.

PartyPoker's mobile app is functional but basic. It lets you play cash games and tournaments on the go, which is all some players need. The experience is serviceable but unremarkable. Compared to GGPoker's mobile-first approach, PartyPoker's app feels like an afterthought.

Payments and Withdrawals

Both rooms accept standard Ontario payment methods, including Visa, Mastercard, Interac, and selected e-wallets. Processing times are similar. Neither room has a significant advantage here, though GGPoker's slightly faster withdrawal processing in our tests gives it a minor edge.

Customer Support

GGPoker offers 24/7 live chat and email support with generally fast response times. PartyPoker provides email and live chat support as well, though response times can be inconsistent based on our testing. GGPoker's support was more reliably responsive in our evaluations.

Rewards Programs

GGPoker's Fish Buffet rewards cash game and tournament play with direct cash rewards. The tiered system is straightforward and offers decent value at every level. The GGCare program adds random cashback on top.

PartyPoker's Diamond Club is designed for higher-volume players and offers cashback at elevated tiers. For the average recreational or low-to-mid volume player, the Diamond Club's requirements may be hard to reach. GGPoker's rewards structure is more accessible and delivers better value for the majority of Ontario players.

Our Verdict: GGPoker Wins

After comparing every major category, GGPoker comes out ahead of PartyPoker in Ontario. The larger player pool means more games running at more stakes. The software is more innovative, with built-in tools that other rooms simply don't offer. The tournament schedule is deeper, the mobile app is better, and the rewards program delivers solid value.

PartyPoker is a legitimate, regulated room with its own strengths, and there's nothing wrong with playing there. But if you're choosing one room as your primary Ontario poker site, GGPoker is the stronger pick.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GGPoker much better than PartyPoker in Ontario?

GGPoker rates significantly higher across most categories, including player pool, software features, tournaments, and mobile app quality. PartyPoker is a decent room, but it trails GGPoker in almost every measurable area.

Does PartyPoker share its player pool?

Yes. PartyPoker shares its Ontario player pool with BetMGM Poker and Bwin. This shared network helps boost traffic, but the combined total still falls short of GGPoker's standalone numbers.

What is fastforward on PartyPoker?

Fastforward is PartyPoker's fast-fold cash game format. When you fold, you're immediately moved to a new table with new opponents. GGPoker offers a similar format called Rush & Cash.

Which room is better for VIP players?

PartyPoker's Diamond Club offers high-volume players a VIP program with cashback rewards. GGPoker's Fish Buffet also rewards volume with cash. For most players, GGPoker's overall package of better traffic, features, and rewards provides more value.
