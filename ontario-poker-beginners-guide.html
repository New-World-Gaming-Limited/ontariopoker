Ontario Online Poker Beginners Guide (2026)

Everything you need to start playing regulated online poker in Ontario. Updated March 2026.

By Maya Chen, Senior Poker Analyst · March 22, 2026

Welcome to Ontario Online Poker

Ontario is one of the best places in the world to play regulated online poker. Since April 2022, six licensed operators have been offering real-money poker to residents and visitors in the province. If you're new to online poker, this guide covers everything from choosing a room to playing your first hand.

Step 1: Understand the Basics

What is Online Poker?

Online poker works exactly like live poker, but played through software on your computer or phone. You play against other real people (not the house), and the operator takes a small fee called "rake" from each pot or tournament entry. Games run around the clock, and you can play multiple tables simultaneously.

Why Ontario?

Ontario's regulated market means every operator is licensed by the Alcohol and Gaming Commission of Ontario (AGCO) and overseen by iGaming Ontario. This gives you:

  • Player protection: Your deposits are safeguarded, and operators must meet strict financial standards.
  • Fair games: Random number generators are independently tested and certified.
  • Dispute resolution: If something goes wrong, you have a formal complaint process through the AGCO.
  • Responsible gambling tools: Deposit limits, session reminders, and self-exclusion are required by law.

Step 3: Create Your Account

The sign-up process is the same across all six Ontario rooms:

  1. Visit the operator's .ca website (e.g., GGPoker.ca, PokerStars.ca).
  2. Click Register/Sign Up and enter your personal details.
  3. Verify your identity. You'll need to provide a government-issued photo ID and proof of address. This KYC (Know Your Customer) process is required by law.
  4. Set up responsible gambling tools. Before depositing, set a deposit limit. Every regulated Ontario room offers this feature. We strongly recommend using it from day one.
  5. Make your first deposit. Most rooms accept Interac, Visa, Mastercard, and e-wallets like PayPal. Minimum deposits are typically $10-$20.

For more detail, see our complete how-to guide.

Step 4: Start at the Lowest Stakes

This is the most important piece of advice for any beginner: start at the lowest stakes available.

  • Cash games: Start at NL2 ($0.01/$0.02 blinds). A $20 deposit gives you 10 buy-ins at this level.
  • Tournaments: Look for $1-$3 buy-in events. Many rooms run freerolls (free-entry tournaments) as well.
  • Sit & Go: $1-$2 Sit & Go or lottery SnG games are a great way to learn tournament dynamics in short sessions.

The goal at micro stakes isn't to make money. It's to learn the software, understand the game flow, and develop fundamental skills without risking meaningful amounts. Move up only after you've demonstrated consistent results over a significant sample.

Step 5: Learn the Fundamentals

Hand Rankings

If you don't know the hand rankings by heart, learn them before playing a single hand. From lowest to highest: High Card, Pair, Two Pair, Three of a Kind, Straight, Flush, Full House, Four of a Kind, Straight Flush, Royal Flush. Our poker glossary covers all the terminology.

Position

Where you sit relative to the dealer button is one of the most important factors in poker. Players who act later in the betting round have more information and can make better decisions. Play tighter from early position and wider from late position.

Starting Hand Selection

As a beginner, play fewer hands and play them aggressively. A simple starting hand chart for NL Hold'em: play premium pairs (AA, KK, QQ, JJ), big suited cards (AK, AQ, AJ, KQ suited), and medium pairs (TT-77). Fold everything else from early position. Expand slightly from late position. Visit our strategy section for more detail.

Bankroll Management

Never play with money you can't afford to lose. For cash games, a standard beginner bankroll is 20-30 buy-ins for your regular stake. For tournaments, 50-100 buy-ins. Use our bankroll calculator to find the right stake for your budget.

Ontario has excellent free resources for learning poker:

  • GGPoker PokerCraft: Built-in analytics tool that tracks your play and shows hand histories. Free for all GGPoker Ontario players.
  • Our tools: Odds calculator, bankroll calculator, rake calculator, and room comparison tool.
  • Freerolls: Free-entry tournaments offered by all Ontario rooms. See our freerolls guide.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  1. Playing too many hands. The #1 leak. Fold more, especially from early position.
  2. Calling too much. Beginners tend to call when they should raise or fold. Be decisive.
  3. Ignoring position. Playing the same range from every seat is a major mistake.
  4. Chasing draws without odds. Don't call a big bet hoping to hit your flush if the odds aren't there. Use our odds calculator to learn pot odds.
  5. Playing above your bankroll. Moving up stakes too quickly is the fastest way to go broke.
  6. Tilting. Bad beats happen. If you feel frustrated, stop playing. Set session time limits using your room's responsible gambling tools.
