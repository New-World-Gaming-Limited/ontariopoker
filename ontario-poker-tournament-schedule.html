Understanding Ontario Tournament Schedules

Each of Ontario's six regulated poker rooms runs its own daily and weekly tournament schedule. Because these rooms operate ring-fenced player pools (Ontario-only), tournament fields are smaller than what you would find on global platforms. This means smaller guarantees but also softer fields in many cases.

GGPoker typically runs the busiest tournament schedule in Ontario, including WSOP Super Circuit events during designated series periods and recurring microFestival events for lower-stakes players. PokerStars Ontario offers a broad daily schedule and special weekend events. 888poker focuses on BLAST jackpot formats and weekly freerolls alongside standard MTTs.

BetMGM Poker, PartyPoker Ontario, and Bwin Ontario run smaller tournament schedules, but can still be worth checking for overlay opportunities when registration does not fill the guarantee.

All buy-in amounts and guarantee sizes should be confirmed directly on the operator's site. This page lists approximate buy-in ranges to give you a general sense of what each room offers.

For Sunday play, which traditionally draws the most tournament traffic, GGPoker and PokerStars Ontario both run their largest weekly events with the highest guarantees. If you only play tournaments one day a week, Sunday offers the best selection across all six rooms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Ontario rooms update their schedules regularly. Daily events tend to stay consistent week to week, but special series, guarantee amounts, and structures change monthly or seasonally. Always confirm the current schedule on the operator's site before planning your sessions.

GGPoker and PokerStars Ontario offer the widest range of daily tournaments. GGPoker runs WSOP Circuit events and microFestival series, while PokerStars claims to have more daily events than any other room. Visit each operator's site for current schedules.

Some rooms offer satellite paths to live events. PokerStars runs Power Path qualifiers and GGPoker offers WSOP Circuit satellites. Check each operator's site for current satellite offerings.

A guarantee (or GTD) is the minimum prize pool the operator commits to for a tournament. If buy-ins do not cover the guarantee, the operator makes up the difference (called an overlay). Guarantees vary and should be confirmed on the operator's site.
