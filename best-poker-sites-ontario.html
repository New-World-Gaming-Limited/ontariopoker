How to Choose the Right Ontario Poker Room

With six regulated rooms to choose from, the best fit depends on your playing style, priorities, and experience level. Here is a breakdown by player type.

Best for Tournament Players

GGPoker leads with WSOP Super Circuit Ontario events and recurring microFestival series, offering some of the largest tournament prize pools available to Ontario players. PokerStars claims more daily tournaments than any other Ontario room, and its Power Path qualifier system connects online play to live PokerStars events. 888poker rounds out the top three for tournament players with its daily schedule plus BLAST jackpot format.

Best for Cash Game Grinders

All six rooms offer No Limit Hold'em and Pot Limit Omaha. The differentiator is the fast-fold format: GGPoker has Rush and Cash, PokerStars has Zoom, 888poker has SNAP, BetMGM has FastForward, and PartyPoker has Fast Forward. For players who value action and volume, fast-fold availability is often the deciding factor.

Best for Beginners

888poker is a strong starting point for new players thanks to its no-deposit tournament tickets over seven days and an extensive weekly freeroll schedule. No promo code is required to join. GGPoker's Honeymoon for Newcomers program provides a 30-day mission structure that eases new players into the ecosystem.

Best for Rewards and Rakeback

BetMGM Poker stands out with Grind Rewards and a visible BRP ladder, providing the clearest rewards structure in the Ontario market. GGPoker's Maple Rewards program includes monthly Ontario-exclusive rewards. PartyPoker offers a cashback rewards program. Visit each operator's site for current reward tier details.

Best Mobile Experience

GGPoker and PokerStars have the most feature-rich mobile apps, with dedicated iOS and Android clients. BetMGM and 888poker also offer native mobile apps. PartyPoker has mobile apps available, while Bwin's poker is accessible via mobile browser. All apps require geolocation verification before play.

Ontario Poker Market Context

Understanding the market helps frame why these six rooms matter. Ontario launched its regulated iGaming market on April 4, 2022, becoming the first Canadian province to allow private operators to compete for players. As of March 16, 2026, the iGO operator directory lists 47 operators, 81 gaming websites, of which six offer live poker tables.

The channelization rate of 83.7% in FY2024-25 means almost all Ontario online gambling happens on regulated sites. For poker specifically, total wagers reached C$1.678 billion in 2025, with NAGGR of C$70.2 million. Poker share of total wagers (~1.7%) and NAGGR (~1.74%) has declined slightly, not because poker is shrinking but because casino and sports betting are growing faster.

For comprehensive market data, see our Ontario poker market size and trends page.

Promo Codes and Welcome Offers

Ontario poker promotions operate under AGCO marketing standards. Advertising and marketing materials that communicate gambling inducements, bonuses, and credits are prohibited except on an operator gaming site. This means many welcome offers are only visible once you visit the operator site directly.

As of March 2026, none of the six regulated Ontario poker rooms require a promo code. Offers are applied directly when you register and make a first deposit on the operator's site. We verify each code against the operator official Ontario-facing pages and update our promo codes hub weekly.

Our Ranking Methodology

We evaluate each poker room across six weighted categories. The overall rating is a composite of these scores:

  • Bonus and Promotions (20%): Welcome offer quality, ongoing promotions, promo code availability, and bonus clearing terms.
  • Software and Features (25%): Desktop and mobile app quality, table features, multi-tabling support, and overall user experience.
  • Tournament Offering (20%): Daily schedule depth, guaranteed prize pools, festival series, and qualifier availability.
  • Cash Games (15%): Format variety, stake range, fast-fold options, and table traffic.
  • Payments (10%): Deposit and withdrawal methods, processing speed, minimum amounts, and KYC experience.
  • Support (10%): Live chat availability, response quality, help centre depth, and dispute handling.

Ratings are updated quarterly or when operators make significant changes to their offering. For full methodology details, see our editorial policy.
