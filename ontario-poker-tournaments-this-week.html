Ontario Poker Tournaments This Week

Last updated March 2026

Ontario's six regulated poker rooms run hundreds of tournaments every week, from low buy-in dailies to high-guarantee Sunday majors. This guide explains the regular weekly schedules so you always know where the action is, no matter what day it is.

For a complete, room-by-room breakdown of every regular tournament, visit our full Ontario poker tournament schedule.

Where to Find This Week's Tournaments

Tournament schedules change as rooms add special events, festival series, and seasonal promotions. The most reliable way to find exactly what's running this week is to check the tournament lobby in each app. Here's how:

  • GGPoker: Open the app and tap "Tournaments." Use the filter to select specific days, buy-in ranges, or formats. GGPoker runs the most tournaments of any Ontario room, typically 50+ events daily. Full GGPoker review.
  • 888poker: Navigate to "Tournament" in the lobby. Filter by game type and buy-in. Their BLAST and Made To Play events run on fixed schedules. Full 888poker review.
  • BetMGM Poker: Check the "Tournaments" tab. BetMGM shares its network with PartyPoker and Bwin, so the same events appear across all three rooms. Full BetMGM review.
  • PokerStars: The "Tourney" lobby offers the most detailed filtering options. You can search by name, buy-in range, start time, and format. Full PokerStars review.
  • PartyPoker: Shares the BetMGM/Bwin network. Check "Tournaments" for the same schedule available on all three skins. Full PartyPoker review.
  • Bwin: Same shared network as PartyPoker and BetMGM. Tournament lobby accessible via desktop client or mobile browser. Full Bwin review.

GGPoker: Largest Weekly Tournament Schedule

GGPoker consistently offers the most tournament variety in Ontario. Their weekly schedule includes progressive knockouts, Spin & Gold satellites, bounty events, and a full Sunday Major lineup. The GGPoker app makes it easy to set tournament reminders and register in advance, even from mobile. For players who prioritize tournaments, GGPoker is the clear first choice.

Sunday Majors: The Biggest Events Each Week

Sunday is the most important day in the Ontario tournament calendar. Every room puts its best guarantees forward. GGPoker's Sunday Main Event and PokerStars' Sunday Special typically draw the largest fields. If you can only play one day a week, Sunday gives you the most action and the biggest potential payouts.

Satellites for Sunday Majors often run throughout the week, giving you a chance to win your seat at a fraction of the direct buy-in. Check each room's satellite schedule starting Monday.

Special Series and Festival Events

Beyond the regular weekly schedule, Ontario rooms frequently run multi-day tournament series with larger prize pools. GGPoker hosts several festival series throughout the year, featuring dozens of events over a one or two-week span. PokerStars and 888poker also run seasonal series tied to their global brands. These series are typically announced a few weeks in advance and include satellites in the days leading up to the main events.

Keep an eye on our news hub for announcements about upcoming series. Festival events often overlay at the lower buy-in levels, creating extra value for players who enter directly rather than through satellites.

Tips for Choosing Tournaments This Week

  • Check for overlay: Early-week events (Monday, Tuesday) often don't reach their guarantee, meaning the prize pool exceeds the total buy-ins collected. This is free money for the field.
  • Time your sessions: Evening tournaments (7pm-10pm ET) draw the biggest fields. Afternoon events tend to be softer.
  • Mix formats: Don't just play freezeouts. PKO (progressive knockout) tournaments let you lock in value with each bounty, reducing variance.
  • Use satellites: If a buy-in is outside your bankroll, look for satellites running 1-3 days before the target event.
  • Bankroll management: A general rule is to keep 50-100 buy-ins for your regular tournament level. Our Bankroll Calculator can help you work out what makes sense for your play volume.

How Late Registration Works

Most Ontario poker tournaments allow late registration, meaning you can enter after the event has started. Late registration windows vary by room and event. GGPoker typically offers generous late reg periods, sometimes extending well into the tournament. This is useful if you miss the start time or want to see how the field shapes up before committing your buy-in. All rooms display the late registration deadline in the tournament lobby.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find Ontario poker tournaments happening this week?

Check the tournament lobbies in each regulated poker app. GGPoker, 888poker, BetMGM, PokerStars, PartyPoker, and Bwin all display upcoming tournaments with start times, buy-ins, and guaranteed prize pools. Our tournament schedule page also tracks weekly events across all rooms.

What days have the biggest Ontario poker tournaments?

Sundays feature the largest guaranteed tournaments across all Ontario rooms. GGPoker and PokerStars run their flagship weekly events on Sunday afternoons and evenings. Saturdays also offer larger-than-average guarantees.

Are there freeroll tournaments in Ontario this week?

Yes. All six regulated Ontario poker rooms run daily or weekly freerolls. GGPoker and 888poker tend to offer the most frequent freeroll schedules. Check each room's tournament lobby for current freeroll listings. See our Ontario freerolls guide for more.

Can I play Ontario poker tournaments on mobile?

Yes. All regulated Ontario rooms allow tournament registration and play through their mobile apps. GGPoker and PokerStars offer the smoothest mobile tournament experience, including late registration from your phone. See our best poker app guide.
